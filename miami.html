The launch city. 7 World Cup matches including a semi-final. Complete neighbourhood guide, Curator deployment plan, and fan nation strategy.

Why Miami Is City #1

Miami hosts 7 World Cup matches at Hard Rock Stadium — more than most other US host cities. The match roster includes games likely featuring Argentina and Brazil, the two biggest fan nations with the largest diaspora communities already living in South Florida. Brazil vs Scotland on June 24 alone will bring more concentrated international fan traffic than any other single group-stage match.

Miami also has the strongest existing infrastructure for international visitors: multilingual population, international airport hub (MIA), established tourism infrastructure, and a Latin American cultural foundation that makes visiting fans from South America feel immediately welcome.

Neighbourhood Guide — For Visitors

Brickell

Best for: Business travelers, finance professionals, upscale dining
Vibe: Manhattan-meets-Miami. High-rise condos, corporate offices, rooftop bars
Key spots: Brickell City Centre (shopping/dining), Quinto La Huella (Argentine restaurant), Komodo (upscale Asian), La Mar (Peruvian)
Transit: Metromover stops at Brickell and Fifth Street. Walking distance to downtown
Stay here if: You want luxury, walkability, and a professional atmosphere

Wynwood

Best for: Young fans, art lovers, food enthusiasts, nightlife seekers
Vibe: Open-air art gallery meets gastro district. Colourful, energetic, photogenic
Key spots: Wynwood Walls (free outdoor murals), KYU (Asian BBQ), Salty Donut (Instagram-famous), NW 2nd Ave restaurant row
Transit: No direct Metrorail. Uber/Lyft or walking from Design District
Stay here if: You want art, street food, craft cocktails, and a creative atmosphere

Little Havana

Best for: Latin American fans, cultural explorers, authentic food seekers
Vibe: The heart of Cuban Miami. Vibrant, authentic, bilingual (Spanish dominant)
Key spots: Calle Ocho (SW 8th Street), Domino Park (Máximo Gómez Park), Versailles Restaurant (Cuban coffee institution), Ball & Chain (live Latin music)
Transit: Bus routes along SW 8th Street. 15-minute drive from downtown
Stay here if: You're a Latin American fan who wants to feel at home immediately

Coconut Grove

Best for: Families, relaxed travelers, waterfront lovers
Vibe: Miami's oldest neighbourhood. Bohemian roots, now upscale-casual
Key spots: Peacock Park (Saturday farmers market), CocoWalk (shopping), Vizcaya Museum, waterfront parks
Transit: Metrorail Coconut Grove station. Walkable neighbourhood once you arrive
Stay here if: You want a calmer, greener Miami experience away from the tourist intensity

Miami Beach

Best for: General tourists, beach access, nightlife (mainstream)
Vibe: The postcard version of Miami. Art Deco architecture, beach, party scene
Key spots: Ocean Drive (walk it, don't eat there — tourist trap pricing), Lincoln Road (outdoor dining), North Beach (quieter, less crowded beaches)
Transit: Causeway bus from downtown. 20-30 minute drive depending on traffic
Stay here if: You want the classic beach experience and don't mind tourist pricing

Design District

Best for: Luxury shoppers, art collectors, fine dining
Vibe: Polished, curated, upscale. The anti-South Beach
Key spots: Louis Vuitton, Prada flagship stores. Michael's Genuine (restaurant), Mandolin (Mediterranean). Institute of Contemporary Art (free)
Transit: Walking distance from Wynwood. No direct Metrorail — drive or ride
Stay here if: You want luxury retail and high-end dining in a less crowded setting

Getting to Hard Rock Stadium

From Downtown / Brickell / Wynwood
Distance: ~17 miles north
Normal drive time: 30-45 minutes
Match day drive time: 90-120+ minutes
Recommendation: Take the Tri-Rail or arrange rideshare 3+ hours before kickoff. Parking at the stadium is available but fills early. Tailgating culture exists — arrive early if driving
DO NOT: Plan to Uber 1 hour before kickoff — surge pricing will be extreme and wait times long

Curator Deployment — Miami

🌴
Latin Culture Curator
Priority Hire #1 · Highest Revenue Potential
Must speak Spanish and ideally Portuguese. Deep knowledge of Little Havana, Doral (Venezuelan/Colombian community), Hialeah (Cuban community). Can connect Brazilian and Argentine fans to the Latin Miami that matches their culture. This is the most valuable Curator role for World Cup because the biggest fan nations (Brazil, Argentina, Colombia, Mexico) are all Spanish/Portuguese speaking.
🍹
Nightlife & Social Curator
Founding Role · Match Night Revenue
Knows which clubs have real energy vs tourist traps. Rooftop bar access. Private event connections. After-party intel. This Curator's revenue will spike on match nights when thousands of fans want to celebrate. Must know Brickell, Wynwood, and South Beach nightlife thoroughly.
🏢
Business & Conference Curator
Founding Role · Enterprise Crossover
For executives attending World Cup matches alongside business trips. Private dining recommendations, meeting spaces, who to know in Miami's business community. This role doubles as the enterprise product — corporate clients pay premium for executive hospitality.
🍽
Food & Restaurant Curator
Founding Role · Highest Volume
The "where should I eat?" expert. Covers everything from Versailles to fine dining. Knows which restaurants to book weeks in advance during World Cup and which hidden gems will still have availability. Food tours across multiple neighbourhoods.
🎨
Art & Culture Curator
Founding Role · Premium Audience
Pérez Art Museum, ICA, Wynwood galleries, Design District. Connects visitors with Miami's art scene beyond the murals. Gallery opening access, artist studio visits, cultural event calendar. Appeals to the higher-spending visitor segment.
