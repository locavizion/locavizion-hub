30-Day Action Plan

Step-by-step founder execution plan with daily tasks, weekly checkpoints, and the deliverables that make Locavizion investor-ready in 30 days.

Week 1 — Foundation (Days 1-7)

Day 1: Website live, SSL confirmed (check green padlock), test chatbot with 5 different questions, verify Google Form submissions reach your Google Sheet
Day 2: Email 10 personal contacts (friends, family, professional contacts) with the locavizion.com link. Ask for honest feedback on: first impression, clarity of the concept, would they sign up, anything confusing
Day 3: Send first Curator outreach message. Target: @tampafoodgirl on Instagram. Reference her most recent post specifically. Use Template 1 from the Outreach Playbook
Day 4: Set up Instagram @locavizion (business account). Post first piece of content: a carousel titled "5 things nobody tells you about Tampa Bay." Follow 50 relevant local accounts
Day 5: Send 3 more Curator outreach messages: @thisbabeeats (food), Embarc Collective email (tech), Creative Pinellas DM (arts). Each one personalised with a specific reference
Day 6: Follow up on Day 3 @tampafoodgirl message if no response. Post second Instagram content: a reel titled "Only locals know about this spot in Ybor City"
Day 7: Week 1 review (use weekly template from Tracker). Count: waitlist signups, outreach messages sent, responses received. Plan Week 2 priorities. First LinkedIn post about Locavizion

Week 2 — Outreach (Days 8-14)

Days 8-9: Send 5 more Curator outreach messages across Tampa, Miami, and NYC. Target at least one person in each city. Use different templates matched to their personality type
Day 10: Complete 3 user interviews. Target: friends or contacts who travel for business or pleasure. Ask: how do they research cities now? What frustrates them? Would they pay for local guidance? Document quotes
Days 11-12: First Curator call — use the 5-minute pitch structure from the Sales Guide. Take notes. If they're interested, send the Curator agreement within 24 hours
Day 13: Submit first accelerator application. Priority: Techstars (check application deadlines at techstars.com/accelerators). Use the vision doc from the 30-Day Action Guide as the narrative
Day 14: Week 2 review. Target by now: 25 waitlist signups, 8+ outreach messages sent, 1+ Curator call completed, 3+ user interviews done. Instagram at 100+ followers

Week 3 — Traction (Days 15-21)

Days 15-17: Sign first 2 Curators. Send agreements, get signatures, create their profiles. Help them design their first experience listing
Days 18-19: Complete 7 more user interviews (10 total). Start seeing patterns in responses. Document common quotes, frustrations, and willingness-to-pay data points
Day 20: Create investor contact list — 30 names. Include: angel investors in Tampa/Miami, travel/hospitality VCs, accelerator partners, personal network connections who know investors. Identify 5 warm intro paths
Day 21: Week 3 review. Target: 40 waitlist signups, 2 Curators signed, 10 user interviews, investor list started. Send first 2 investor briefs via Docsend

Week 4 — Investor Ready (Days 22-30)

Days 22-24: Send pitch deck to 5 warm investor contacts via Docsend. Include a brief email: 3 sentences about what Locavizion is, one stat (World Cup visitors), one proof point (X Curators signed, Y waitlist), and the Docsend link
Days 25-26: Complete remaining user interviews (20 total). Compile quotes, stats, and insights into a 1-page user research summary
Days 27-28: Submit 3 more accelerator applications (4 total). Prioritise programs with travel, hospitality, or sports tech focus
Days 29-30: Final week review. Compile 30-day results into a summary doc. Update pitch deck with real traction numbers. Celebrate what you've built
The 30-Day Outcome

By Day 30 you should have: a vision doc, a live waitlist with 50+ signups, 5 curator conversations documented, 20 user interviews with quotes and data, 4 accelerator applications submitted, and a 30-name investor list with 5 warm intro paths identified. That is a fundable story. That is a founder who moves. No money spent. No product built. Just evidence that you are the right person to build this.
