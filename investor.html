Executive Summary

Locavizion is a hospitality intelligence platform that connects travelers with verified local Curators for personalised city experiences. Unlike travel review sites that aggregate anonymous opinions, Locavizion matches visitors with domain-specific experts — food, nightlife, business networking, culture, outdoors — who provide AI-enhanced briefings and bookable experiences.

The platform launches during the 2026 FIFA World Cup, the largest sporting event in history, which brings an estimated 10 million international visitors to 11 US host cities over 39 days. This creates a once-in-a-generation customer acquisition window where millions of people actively need exactly what Locavizion provides: local knowledge in an unfamiliar city.

The Product

Three integrated layers:

  • Layer 1 — AI City Guide (free) — Claude-powered chatbot trained on Curator knowledge. Answers questions about neighbourhoods, restaurants, transit, safety, cultural context. Available 24/7, covers all active cities. Serves as the top-of-funnel that drives sign-ups and Curator bookings
  • Layer 2 — Curator Briefings (paid) — 1:1 video briefings with verified local Curators. 30-60 minutes. Traveler shares their trip details, Curator provides a personalised city plan with specific restaurant reservations to make, neighbourhoods to stay in, events happening during their visit, and people to meet. $50–$150 per session depending on the Curator and city
  • Layer 3 — Bookable Experiences (paid) — Curators design and host group experiences: food tours, nightlife guides, cultural walks, business networking events, neighbourhood deep-dives. $50–$200 per person. Groups of 4-12. Curators set their own pricing, schedule, and experience design

Why 70/30 Curator Split

Most tour platforms (GetYourGuide, Viator, Airbnb Experiences) take 20-30% but provide commoditised, undifferentiated listings. Food delivery apps take 30%+. Locavizion's 70/30 split is deliberately generous because:

  • Curator retention is everything — A great Curator generates repeat bookings and referrals. Losing one costs more than the 10% margin difference
  • Quality attracts quality — Generous economics attract better Curators, which creates better experiences, which drives more members, which benefits everyone
  • The platform makes money on volume — 30% of a $120 experience × 50 bookings/month per Curator × 15 Curators = $27,000/month platform revenue from experiences alone
