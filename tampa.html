Tampa Bay's World Cup Opportunity

Tampa is a World Cup host city with matches at Raymond James Stadium — but beyond the stadium, visitors have almost zero context for what Tampa Bay actually offers. Unlike Miami (internationally famous) or NYC (globally recognised), Tampa Bay is a relative unknown to international visitors. That's both the challenge and the opportunity.

The challenge: Visitors won't know to explore Ybor City, St. Pete, or the waterfront unless someone tells them.
The opportunity: Every single visitor needs guidance. There's no "I already know Tampa" crowd. The addressable market is 100% of visitors.

Key Areas

Ybor City

Tampa's historic Latin quarter, founded in the 1880s by cigar manufacturers. The most culturally significant neighbourhood for World Cup visitors, especially Latin American fans.

  • Columbia Restaurant — Oldest restaurant in Florida (1905). Spanish-Cuban cuisine. Multiple dining rooms with Moorish tile work. A must-visit for every visitor
  • Seventh Avenue (La Séptima) — Main strip. Bars, restaurants, clubs, cigar shops. Saturday night is the biggest night. Walking distance end-to-end
  • Circulo Cubano — Historic Cuban social club. Cultural events, community gatherings. One of several historic social clubs (Italian Club, Spanish Club) that tell the immigration story
  • Cigar shops — Several shops on 7th Ave still hand-roll cigars. Visitors can watch the process and buy fresh cigars. Unique to Tampa — this tradition exists almost nowhere else in America
  • Nightlife — Gaspar's Grotto, The Castle, Club Prana. Ybor has Tampa's densest nightlife district. Safe, walkable, well-lit. Police presence on weekends

SoHo (South Howard Avenue)

Tampa's restaurant row. The best dining density in the city.

  • Howard Avenue between Bayshore and Kennedy — 20+ restaurants within a 6-block walk. Perfect for a food crawl
  • Key restaurants: Datz (American eclectic), Bulla Gastrobar (Spanish tapas), The Refinery (seasonal American), Ciro's Speakeasy (hidden cocktail bar)
  • Vibe: Young professional, 25-40 age range. Casual upscale. Walkable from Hyde Park
  • Walking distance: Hyde Park Village (boutique shopping), Bayshore Boulevard

Downtown / Water Street Tampa

  • Tampa Riverwalk — 2.6-mile waterfront walking path. Connects museums, parks, restaurants, and the convention center. Excellent for morning runs and evening strolls
  • Sparkman Wharf — Waterfront dining and entertainment complex. Container-style restaurants, outdoor seating, bay views
  • Water Street Tampa — Massive new mixed-use development by Jeff Vinik (Lightning owner). Hotels, restaurants, offices. The Tampa Edition hotel is the flagship
  • Proximity to stadium: Raymond James Stadium is ~2 miles from downtown. Walkable or short ride on match days

St. Petersburg

  • Salvador Dali Museum — World's largest collection of Dali works outside Spain. Architecturally stunning building. A must-visit even for non-art fans
  • Central Avenue — Main street of St. Pete. Independent shops, galleries, craft breweries (Green Bench, 3 Daughters, Cycle Brewing). Walking distance from the waterfront
  • First Friday Art Walk — Monthly event on the first Friday. Galleries open late, live music, street vendors, food trucks. Thousands attend. The best introduction to St. Pete's creative community
  • Beaches: St. Pete Beach and Treasure Island. Among the best-rated beaches in the US. 20-minute drive from downtown St. Pete
  • Distance: 30 minutes from Tampa across the Howard Frankland Bridge. Consider it a separate city visit, not a quick side trip

Bayshore Boulevard & Davis Islands

  • Bayshore Boulevard — 4.5 miles of continuous waterfront sidewalk. The world's longest. Stunning bay views, especially at sunrise and sunset. Running, cycling, walking, rollerblading
  • Davis Islands — Small residential island community just south of downtown. Peter O. Knight Airport (small private airport), Davis Islands Beach, yacht clubs. Old Florida charm
  • Best for: Morning runners, photographers, anyone who wants to see Tampa's waterfront beauty

Year-Round Opportunity

Tampa's World Cup matches are the launch event, but the year-round opportunity is substantial:

  • New residents: Tampa adds thousands of transplants monthly — remote workers, military families, retirees, tech workers. All need local orientation
  • Business conferences: Tampa Convention Center hosts major events year-round. Every attendee is a potential Locavizion user
  • Cruise port: Port Tampa Bay is a major cruise departure point. Pre/post-cruise city exploration is a natural use case
  • Spring training: MLB spring training brings 1M+ visitors to the Tampa Bay area every February-March
  • University influx: USF (50,000+ students) brings new residents every fall semester
Tampa Differentiator

Unlike Miami or NYC, Tampa is not overcrowded with competitors. There are no well-known "local guide" platforms for Tampa. GetYourGuide has minimal Tampa listings. Airbnb Experiences coverage is thin. Locavizion can own Tampa Bay's local intelligence market with minimal competition.
