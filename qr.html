QR Codes & Sticker Designs

Printable guerrilla marketing assets — QR stickers, flyers, bar/restaurant table cards, and placement strategy for Tampa Bay, Miami, and NYC.

Why Guerrilla Marketing Works for Locavizion

Digital ads are expensive and generic. A QR sticker at the right restaurant reaches exactly the right person — someone already in the city, already exploring, already open to local recommendations. The conversion rate on a well-placed QR code in a relevant location is dramatically higher than any Instagram ad.

Cost comparison: 500 QR stickers = $45. One week of Instagram ads reaching the same audience = $200+. And the sticker stays there for months.

QR Code Placements — By Location Type

1. Restaurant Table Cards

  • What: Small 3×5 tent cards placed on tables at partner restaurants
  • Copy: "New to [city]? Scan for your free AI city guide" + QR code + locavizion.com
  • Targets: Tourist-friendly restaurants in Brickell, Wynwood, Ybor City, SoHo Tampa, Times Square area
  • Ask: Offer the restaurant a free Featured Venue listing in exchange for table card placement
  • Cost: 200 tent cards ≈ $30 on Vistaprint

2. Hotel Lobby Displays

  • What: Small acrylic display with QR card at concierge desk or elevator bank
  • Copy: "See [city] like a local — free AI city guide" + QR
  • Targets: Boutique hotels and Airbnb-managed buildings. Avoid major chains (require corporate approval)
  • Ask: Offer the hotel a partnership listing. Many boutique hotels love offering guests unique local experiences

3. Coffee Shop Counter Cards

  • What: Small cards near the register or on the bar counter
  • Copy: "Ask a local, not Google" + QR
  • Targets: Independent coffee shops in creative neighbourhoods (Wynwood, St. Pete, Williamsburg)
  • Ask: Just ask the barista. Most indie coffee shops are happy to put a small card near the register

4. Airport Arrival Areas

  • What: Handheld flyers or small posters in the rideshare pickup zone
  • Copy: "Just landed? Get your free [city] guide before you leave the airport" + QR
  • Targets: TPA (Tampa), MIA and FLL (Miami), JFK and EWR (NYC)
  • Legal note: Airport authorities may remove unofficial materials. Focus on rideshare pickup zones and nearby parking areas which are less monitored

5. Co-working Spaces

  • What: A4 poster on the community board + counter cards
  • Copy: "New to [city]? Your AI-powered local guide" + QR + "Built for remote workers, digital nomads, and new residents"
  • Targets: Embarc Collective, Station House, WeWork locations, Lab Ybor, The LAB Miami
  • Ask: Most co-working spaces have community boards where members can post

6. World Cup Fan Zone Entrances

  • What: Handheld flyers distributed to fans entering fan zones or heading to matches
  • Copy: "⚽ Your World Cup city guide — free" + QR + "Find the best food, bars & parties near every match"
  • Timing: 2-3 hours before each match. Position at transit stations near the stadium
  • Team: You + 2-3 friends/hired helpers. Wear Locavizion branded shirts

QR Code Generation

Use qr-code-generator.com (free) or Canva (free, includes design tools). Point all QR codes to locavizion.com. If you want to track which placements drive the most scans, create separate QR codes for each location type pointing to URL variants: locavizion.com?ref=restaurant, locavizion.com?ref=hotel, etc. Google Analytics will show you which ref sources perform best.

Design Guidelines

  • Background: Dark (#0c0b09) — matches the brand and stands out on light surfaces
  • QR code: Gold (#c49a2a) on dark background, or dark on gold background for light surfaces
  • Font: Outfit for body text, Cormorant Garamond for "Locavizion" brand name
  • Logo: Include the eye mark at top or bottom, small but recognisable
  • Minimum info: QR code + one line of copy + locavizion.com. Don't overcrowd
