Full Website Mockup

Complete 10-section website design — the full product vision for locavizion.com when it transitions from waitlist to full platform.

Section 1 — Navigation

Sticky header that starts transparent over the hero and transitions to a solid dark background with a subtle gold bottom border as the user scrolls past 100px. Contains:

  • Eye+Globe SVG logo — The Locavizion mark: a compass-like eye icon with a gold iris, surrounded by meridian lines suggesting a globe. 36px square, rendered in SVG for crisp display at any resolution
  • Brand name — "LOCAVIZION" in Cormorant Garamond, 600 weight, 0.2em letter-spacing, gold (#c49a2a)
  • Nav links — How It Works, Cities, Curators, Pricing — each scrolls to the corresponding section with smooth scroll behaviour
  • CTA button — "Join Waitlist" gold button, right-aligned, links to the signup form section
  • Mobile hamburger — On screens under 768px, nav links collapse into a slide-down mobile menu. CTA stays visible

Section 2 — Hero

Full viewport height (100vh). Dark background (#0c0b09) with a subtle radial gold gradient glow (15% opacity) emanating from the upper right and a sage green glow from the lower left. Film grain overlay at 4% opacity using an inline SVG noise pattern.

  • Headline — "See The World Like A Local" — Cormorant Garamond, 3.5rem on desktop, 2.2rem on mobile, 300 weight. Line height 1.15. White with the word "Local" in gold italic
  • Subheadline — "Expert-led experiences and AI-powered intelligence for travelers who want the real version of every city they visit." — Outfit, 1rem, 400 weight, 55% white opacity
  • Two CTAs — Primary gold filled button "Join the Founding Waitlist →" and secondary outline button "See how it works"
  • Stats row — Four stats in a horizontal row: "3 Cities Active Now" · "78 Days to World Cup" (live countdown) · "10M+ World Cup Visitors" · "5 Curator Domains per City". Each stat has a large number in Cormorant Garamond gold and a small label in DM Mono uppercase
  • Scroll indicator — Minimal animated line with "Scroll" text at bottom center, fading in after 2 seconds
  • Entry animation — Headline fades up from 30px below with 0.8s ease, subheadline follows at 0.3s delay, CTAs at 0.5s, stats at 0.7s. CSS-only using @keyframes and animation-delay

Section 3 — The Problem

Light warm background section (#f0ead8) with dark text. Introduces the three core problems Locavizion solves.

  • Eyebrow — "THE PROBLEM" in DM Mono, 0.6rem, gold, 0.2em letter-spacing
  • Headline — "Travelers arrive everywhere. They experience almost nothing." — Cormorant Garamond, 2rem, dark ink, italic emphasis on second line
  • Lead paragraph — Explains the gap between the city search engines know and the city locals know
  • Three problem cards in a grid:
    🗺 "Generic guides miss everything that matters" — TripAdvisor, Google, Yelp optimise for volume, not quality. The same 15 tourist restaurants appear in every city. Real local knowledge never surfaces.
    ⏱ "Business travelers waste their limited time" — Conference attendees fly in for 72 hours, eat at the hotel, visit one TripAdvisor restaurant, and leave. They want the real city but have no guide and no time to find it.
    🏘 "New residents feel like outsiders for years" — Tampa adds thousands of new residents monthly. Miami and NYC receive constant international arrivals. Without a local network, it takes years to feel like you belong. Community shouldn't require 3 years of residency.

Section 4 — How It Works

Three-step horizontal process with connecting lines between steps:

  • Step 1: Ask the AI — "Tell our AI city guide where you're going and what you care about. It instantly surfaces neighbourhood intelligence, timing advice, and cultural context that no search engine knows." Icon: chat bubble with sparkle
  • Step 2: Meet Your Curator — "Get matched with a verified local Curator who specialises in exactly what you need — food, nightlife, business connections, culture, or the outdoors. Book a 1:1 briefing or join a group experience." Icon: person with location pin
  • Step 3: Experience the City — "Show up knowing what a 10-year local knows. Skip the tourist traps, find the hidden gems, and experience the city the way it's meant to be experienced." Icon: compass rose

Section 5 — Platform Features

Tabbed interface with three tabs, each showing a different product layer with a live mock UI preview:

Tab 1: AI City Guide

Mock chat interface showing a conversation. The mock UI shows the chat bubble styling, typing indicator, and suggested follow-up chips.

Tab 2: Curator Matching

Three Curator profile cards displayed in a grid. Each card shows: a circular avatar, the Curator's name, their domain (e.g. "Food & Restaurants · Tampa Bay"), a rating, number of experiences hosted, a 2-line bio, and domain tags (e.g. "Cuban food" · "Seafood" · "Hidden gems"). Below the cards: a "Find Your Curator" button.

Tab 3: Bookable Experiences

Experience listing cards showing: a placeholder image area, experience title (e.g. "Hidden Wynwood: Art, Food & Culture Walk"), the Curator hosting it, duration (3 hours), group size (max 8), price ($85/person), and a "Book Now" button. Three experiences displayed covering food tours, nightlife, and cultural walks.
