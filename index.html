The Opportunity
FIFA World Cup 2026 brings up to 10 million international visitors to the United States — the largest, most complex sporting event in history. The US alone is projected to see a $17.2 billion GDP boost and 185,000 jobs created.
These visitors face a universal problem: they arrive in unfamiliar American cities with no cultural context, no local network, and only generic tourist recommendations. Locavizion solves this by connecting them to verified local Curators who provide personalized city intelligence — neighbourhoods, food, culture, business connections — through AI-enhanced briefings and bookable experiences.

Curator Sales Guide
How to pitch, negotiate, onboard, and retain Curators across all cities.
The 5-Minute Pitch Structure
  • Hook (30 sec) — "The World Cup is bringing 10M visitors. They all need local guidance."
  • Problem (60 sec) — "Right now they use Google, TripAdvisor, generic guides. Nobody's getting real local knowledge."
  • Solution (60 sec) — "Locavizion matches visitors with verified local Curators — people like you."
  • Their Role (60 sec) — "You set your rate, choose your schedule, host experiences you design."
  • The Economics (60 sec) — "You keep 70% of bookings, 65% of briefings. Most platforms take 50%+."
  • The Urgency (30 sec) — "Only 5 founding positions per city. World Cup kicks off June 11."
Common Objections & Responses
"How do I know you'll actually get me bookings?"
"The World Cup alone guarantees the audience. 10 million international visitors, most arriving with zero local connections. Our AI matches them to you based on their specific interests. You're not waiting for random customers — they're being sent to you."
"I already do this independently."
"Perfect — that means you already know the value. We just give you a bigger audience and handle all the admin: payments, scheduling, marketing, customer support. You focus on what you're great at."
"What happens after the World Cup?"
"The World Cup is our launch window, not our entire business. After the tournament, we expand to year-round travel, conferences, and business visitors. Founding Curators keep their position permanently."

Tampa Bay's World Cup Angle
Tampa is a host city with matches at Raymond James Stadium. But beyond the stadium, visitors have almost zero context for what Tampa Bay actually offers. That's the opportunity — every visitor needs a local guide, and nobody is providing one.

World Cup
New York City Blueprint
MetLife Stadium, Manhattan neighbourhoods, Brooklyn culture, transit guide.
NYC World Cup Context
MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, NJ hosts World Cup matches — but fans stay in Manhattan, Brooklyn, and Queens. The disconnect between stadium location and city experience is enormous. Visitors need local guidance more than anywhere else in the tournament.
Key Neighbourhoods for Fans
  • Midtown Manhattan — Hotel hub, Times Square fan zones, transit access to MetLife via NJ Transit
  • Lower East Side — Nightlife, independent restaurants, younger fan crowd
  • Williamsburg, Brooklyn — Creative scene, rooftop bars, street food, Airbnb density
  • Jackson Heights, Queens — The most ethnically diverse neighbourhood in the world. Latin American, South Asian, and African communities. The real NYC.
  • Harlem — Cultural heritage, gospel brunches, jazz clubs, African diaspora connections
  • DUMBO / Brooklyn Heights — Waterfront views, upscale dining, Instagram-worthy locations
Transit to MetLife
From Manhattan
NJ Transit from Penn Station to MetLife Stadium station. ~30 minutes. Trains run frequently on match days. Do NOT take a car — traffic will be catastrophic. Buy NJ Transit tickets in advance via the app.
Fan Zone Locations
Expect FIFA Fan Fest in Times Square or Hudson Yards. Additional fan zones likely in Brooklyn (Prospect Park) and potentially Flushing Meadows (Queens). Official locations TBA.

  • Queens Diversity Curator — Ethnic food tours, community events, the "real" New York

  • Nightlife Curator — Club access, rooftop bars, after-parties, underground events
