Stream 1 — Experience Bookings (70/30)

The core revenue driver. Curators design and host group experiences — food tours, nightlife guides, cultural walks, business networking events, neighbourhood deep-dives. Travelers book through the platform. Payment is collected upfront by Stripe, held in escrow, and settled to the Curator within 3 business days after the experience is completed.

Example Calculation

Food Tour · 8 guests · $95/person
Gross revenue: 8 × $95 = $760.00
Stripe fee (2.9% + $0.30): $22.34
Net after Stripe: $737.66
Curator (70%): $516.36
Platform (30%): $221.30

The Curator walks away with $516 for a 3-hour tour. If they host this twice a week, that's over $4,000/month from a single experience type.

Stream 2 — 1:1 Curator Briefings (65/35)

Video calls where a Curator creates a personalised city plan for a traveler. The traveler shares their trip details (dates, interests, budget, group size) and the Curator builds a custom itinerary with specific restaurant recommendations, neighbourhood advice, event suggestions, and insider tips. Sessions run 30-60 minutes.

Example Calculation

Briefing Session · $95
Gross: $95.00
Stripe fee: $3.06
Net: $91.94
Curator (65%): $59.76
Platform (35%): $32.18

Why 65/35 instead of 70/30: Briefings require the platform to handle scheduling, video infrastructure, matching, and quality follow-up. The extra 5% covers this operational overhead.

Streams 4-6 — Enterprise, Venues, Data

  • Enterprise ($500+/mo) — White-label city intelligence for conferences, corporate retreats, executive hospitality. Custom Curator assignments. Billed monthly or annually by invoice
  • Venue Partnerships ($200-500/mo) — Restaurants, hotels, and venues pay for featured placement in AI guide responses and Curator recommendations. Curators receive referral bonuses for verified visits
  • Data Licensing (custom) — Anonymised visitor behaviour data, city intelligence reports, neighbourhood popularity trends. Sold to tourism boards, real estate developers, hospitality groups

How to Explain the Model

To Curators

"You keep 70% of every experience you host and 65% of every briefing you give. We handle payments, marketing, scheduling, customer support, and the platform. You bring your knowledge and your personality. Most food tour companies take 50%+ — we give you the majority because your expertise is the product. There are no upfront costs, no monthly fees, no minimums. You earn when you work."

To Investors

"We take 30% of experience bookings, 35% of briefings, and 100% of subscriptions. The Curator-generous split is deliberate — it attracts and retains the highest-quality Curators, which creates better experiences, which drives member growth and retention. We make money on volume, not margin compression. At scale, with 50+ Curators across 11 cities, the platform revenue compounds while Curator acquisition costs decrease through word-of-mouth and referrals."

To Travelers

"You pay for the experience or briefing directly. The price you see is the price you pay. Your Curator receives the majority of that payment because we believe the person sharing their knowledge with you deserves to be compensated well. That's how we attract the best locals in every city."
Key Principle

Curators are not contractors — they are the product. Every pricing decision should be made with this in mind. If a pricing choice saves the platform 5% but risks losing a great Curator, it's the wrong choice. Curator retention drives everything else.
