5 Content Pillars

Pillar 1 — City Intelligence

The core of the brand. Posts that prove Locavizion has knowledge worth paying for.

  • "5 things nobody tells you about Miami" carousels
  • Neighbourhood breakdowns — "Wynwood vs Design District: Where should you actually stay?"
  • Hidden gem reveals — specific restaurants, bars, parks that tourists never find
  • "Locals only" tips — the real version of each city that Google doesn't know
  • Transit guides — "How to actually get to Hard Rock Stadium without sitting in traffic for 3 hours"

Pillar 2 — World Cup Countdown

Time-sensitive content that creates urgency and taps into World Cup search traffic.

  • Daily/weekly countdown posts — "82 days until kickoff. Is your city ready?"
  • Match preview posts — "Brazil vs Scotland, June 24, Miami. Here's what Brazilian fans need to know"
  • City preparation updates — "Miami's fan zone locations announced" (when available)
  • Fan travel tips — "Flying into MIA vs FLL — which airport for which matches"

Pillar 3 — Curator Spotlights

Human faces of the brand. Shows that real people power the platform.

  • Short video intros of each Curator — 30-60 second reels showing their personality and domain
  • "Day in the life" stories showing Curators in their element — at a restaurant, at a gallery, on the water
  • Curator recommendations — "Our Tampa Food Curator's top 3 this week"
  • Behind-the-scenes of experience design — "How Sofia designs a Little Havana food walk"

Pillar 4 — Behind the Build

Founder journey content. Building in public. Startup transparency.

  • Weekly updates — "Week 3: First Curator signed, 47 waitlist signups"
  • Lessons learned — "What I got wrong about travel apps"
  • Milestone celebrations — "100 waitlist signups" (screenshot of Google Sheet with names blurred)
  • Honest posts — "Nobody replied to my first 5 Curator messages. Here's what I changed"

Pillar 5 — Fan Nation Guides

Content targeted at specific fan nations visiting the US for the World Cup.

  • Brazilian fans — "Where to find açaí, feijoada, and samba in Miami"
  • Argentine fans — "The best steak in Tampa Bay (yes, it exists)"
  • Mexican fans — "Where to watch matches with fellow fans in NYC"
  • English fans — "A proper pub guide for every World Cup host city"
  • German fans — "Craft beer in Tampa Bay — a guide for Bundesliga fans"

Growth Tactics

  • Comment strategy: Spend 15 min/day commenting meaningfully on posts from local food bloggers, travel accounts, and World Cup pages. Not generic comments — specific, value-adding responses that make people click your profile
  • Collab posts: Once Curators are signed, create collab posts that appear on both accounts. Instant audience sharing
  • Reels hooks: First 3 seconds decide everything. Start with "Nobody tells you this about [city]…" or "POV: you actually know where to eat in Miami"
  • DM strategy: When someone comments asking for a recommendation, DM them directly with a personalised response + link to waitlist. Convert engagement into signups
  • Cross-posting: Repurpose carousels as LinkedIn posts (different audience, same content). Repurpose reels as TikToks
