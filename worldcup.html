5-Phase Activation Timeline

Phase 1 — Foundation (Now → April 2026)

  • Recruit and sign 15 founding Curators across Tampa, Miami, and NYC (5 per city)
  • Build Curator onboarding materials and experience design templates
  • Launch Instagram @locavizion with consistent content across all 5 pillars
  • Submit accelerator applications (Techstars, Y Combinator, local programs)
  • Begin investor outreach with briefing doc and pitch deck
  • Create fan nation guides for top 10 visiting countries

Phase 2 — Pre-Launch (April → May 2026)

  • Beta test platform with early waitlist signups — free Curator briefings in exchange for feedback
  • Instagram ramp to 1,000+ followers with daily content
  • Secure 5-10 venue partnerships (restaurants, hotels willing to display QR codes)
  • Mobile app MVP in development (React Native, iOS + Android)
  • First press outreach — local media in Tampa, Miami, NYC about World Cup preparation
  • Print and distribute QR stickers, table cards, and flyers across Tampa Bay

Phase 3 — Launch (May → June 11, 2026)

  • Full marketing push — Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, local media, podcast appearances
  • PR campaign — "How a Tampa founder is solving the World Cup's local knowledge problem"
  • Fan nation guide distribution — partner with fan clubs, cultural organisations, Facebook groups
  • Guerrilla campaign — QR flyers at airports, fan zone entrances, hotel lobbies
  • Curator readiness check — all experiences listed, all briefing slots open, all profiles optimised
  • Mobile app beta launch to waitlist members

Phase 4 — Game Time (June 11 → July 19, 2026)

  • Live operations — real-time city intelligence updates, match-day logistics, fan zone coverage
  • Curator support — daily check-ins, booking management, issue resolution
  • Content machine — 3+ Instagram posts per day, stories from every match city, Curator experience recaps
  • Data collection — track bookings, user behaviour, chatbot queries, popular neighbourhoods, Curator performance
  • Customer success — follow up with every booker, collect reviews and testimonials
  • Media engagement — share user stories, Curator spotlights, city intelligence with press

Phase 5 — Post-Cup & Transition (July 20+ )

  • Retain World Cup members — transition to year-round membership with ongoing city intelligence
  • Expand to remaining 8 WC host cities based on Phase 4 learnings
  • Transition Curators to year-round model — conferences, business travel, new residents
  • Raise Series A based on World Cup traction data
  • Build partnerships with convention centres, corporate travel managers, relocation companies
  • International expansion planning — 2028 World Cup (NA again?) and beyond
