Revenue model deep dive, partner tier structure, enterprise pricing strategy, and venue partnership playbook.

6 Revenue Streams — Priority Order

Stream 1: Subscriptions (Immediate)

The baseline recurring revenue. Converts free AI guide users into paying members who want deeper access.

Stream 2: Experience Bookings (Core)

Curators design and host group experiences. Platform takes 30%, Curator gets 70%. This is where the biggest per-transaction revenue comes from. A single sold-out food tour generates $228 in platform revenue ($760 gross × 30%).

Stream 3: Curator Briefings (High Margin)

1:1 video calls, 30-60 minutes. Platform takes 35%, Curator gets 65%. Lower per-transaction value than experiences but higher margin and zero logistics cost. Scalable with no physical constraints.

Stream 4: Enterprise Contracts (Whale Revenue)

Monthly or annual SaaS contracts with corporate clients. 100% platform revenue. Target: conference organisers who want to offer attendees a city intelligence package, corporate travel departments, executive hospitality services, relocation companies.

Stream 5: Venue Partnerships (Passive)

Restaurants, hotels, bars, and venues pay for featured placement in AI guide responses and Curator recommendations.

Stream 6: Data Licensing (Future)

Anonymised visitor behaviour data sold to tourism boards, real estate developers, and hospitality groups. What neighbourhoods are trending? What cuisines are most requested? Where do visitors stay longest? This data is valuable to city planners and commercial developers.

Partner Tiers

Tier 1 — Featured Venue ($200/month)
What they get:
• Restaurant/bar/hotel appears as a recommended spot in AI guide responses for relevant queries
• Listed on Curator briefing recommendation templates
• Profile page with photos, menu highlights, hours, and booking link
• Monthly analytics report: how many times recommended, click-through rate

Best for: Independent restaurants, boutique hotels, cocktail bars, cultural venues
Sales pitch: "When 10,000 World Cup visitors ask our AI 'where should I eat near the stadium?' — your restaurant appears by name."
Tier 2 — City Partner ($500/month)
What they get:
• Everything in Tier 1
• Co-branded city guide section ("Powered by [Partner Name]")
• Featured placement in fan nation guides (e.g. "Brazilian fans: eat here")
• Bulk experience booking capability for their own customers
• Quarterly city intelligence report

Best for: Hotel chains, tourism boards, event venues, convention centres
Sales pitch: "Your property becomes the recommended stay for every visitor who matches your profile."
Tier 3 — Enterprise Partner ($2,000+/month)
What they get:
• White-label Locavizion integration (your brand, our intelligence)
• Custom Curator assignments for VIP guests or conference attendees
• Private briefing sessions and exclusive experiences for your clients
• Full data access: visitor behaviour, booking patterns, city intelligence
• Dedicated account manager

Best for: Corporate travel companies, luxury hospitality groups, conference management firms, relocation services
Sales pitch: "Give every person who walks through your doors the same city intelligence a 10-year local has — without hiring a concierge."

Venue Partnership Playbook

  • Step 1: Identify 20 ideal venue partners per city — restaurants, hotels, bars that Curators already recommend naturally
  • Step 2: Offer the first 5 venues a free 3-month trial. Frame it as "be part of our World Cup launch — free featured placement for founding partners"
  • Step 3: After 3 months, show them analytics: "We recommended your restaurant 847 times to World Cup visitors. X visitors said they came because of Locavizion. Here's what the paid tier looks like"
  • Step 4: Convert free trials to $200/month paid partnerships. 5 venues × $200 = $1,000/month passive revenue per city
  • Step 5: Scale to 20 venues per city across 11 cities = $44,000/month venue partnership revenue at full scale
Revenue Stacking

The power of Locavizion's model is that every stream compounds the others. More members = more Curator bookings = more venue partnership value = better data = higher enterprise contract value. Each new member makes every revenue stream more valuable.
